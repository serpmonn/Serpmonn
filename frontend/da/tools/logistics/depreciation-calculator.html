    FAQ

    How is depreciation per 1 km calculated?

    The calculator estimates total wear from age and mileage with usage adjustments, then divides total depreciation by current mileage to get an approximate cost per kilometre.

    Is this a market appraisal?

    No. It is an approximate planning estimate, not a dealer, bank or insurer valuation.

    Which fields are required?

    Year, initial price and current mileage. Extra fields refine the estimate but are optional.

    Why does commercial use increase wear?

    Taxi, delivery and fleet use usually mean higher annual mileage and harder conditions, so the wear factor is higher.

    Can I save calculations?

    Yes. After you press Calculate, the result is stored in local history on this device. You can reload or delete entries.

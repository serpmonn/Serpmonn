1) What UTM is and why it matters

UTM parameters in a URL help analytics tools (GA4 and others) identify where traffic comes from, what channel type it is, and which campaign/creative was used. This enables correct attribution and channel comparison.

2) Core UTM parameters

  • utm_source — the source (e.g., google, facebook, vk, telegram).
  • utm_medium — channel type (cpc, paid_social, display, email, referral, etc.).
  • utm_campaign — campaign identifier (e.g., y2025q3_launch_widget).
  • utm_term — keyword (commonly in search ads).
  • utm_content — creative variant/button (banner_a_red, cta_top).
Optional: utm_id, utm_source_platform, utm_creative_format, utm_marketing_tactic — GA4 supports them in extended reports.

3) GA4 linkage

GA4 maps UTM into session_source/medium, first_user_source/medium, campaign, etc. Auto‑tagging (gclid) still plays nicely with UTM for cross‑platform consistency and BI.

  • Stick to lowercase and ASCII to avoid channel duplicates.
  • Do not mix channel types in utm_medium (cpc vs paid_social).
  • Ensure redirects do not strip parameters.

4) Taxonomy & naming

  • Campaign: y2025q3_{product|offer}_{goal|event}
  • Case: lowercase only; - as a separator.
  • No spaces; transliterate non‑ASCII if needed.
  • Medium dictionary: cpc, paid_social, display, email, referral, organic.

5) Channel templates

  • Google Ads (Search/Display): utm_source=google, utm_medium=cpc, utm_campaign=..., utm_term={keyword}, utm_content={adgroup}. Keep gclid on.
  • Facebook/Meta: utm_source=facebook, utm_medium=paid_social, utm_campaign=..., utm_content={placement}_{creative}.
  • VK: utm_source=vk, utm_medium=paid_social, utm_campaign=..., utm_content={ad_id}.
  • Telegram: utm_source=telegram, utm_medium=social (or paid_social), utm_campaign=..., utm_content=post_{n}|story.
  • Email: utm_source=newsletter (or ESP), utm_medium=email, utm_campaign=..., utm_content=btn_{variant}.

6) Examples

7) Common mistakes

  • Mixing utm_medium values and casings (CPC vs cpc).
  • Spaces/non‑ASCII in values, missing URL encoding.
  • Different campaign names across platforms.
  • UTM lost on redirects (opaque shorteners, misconfigured intermediates).

8) FAQ & checklist

What is the minimum set?

source, medium, campaign — enough to start; add term/content when needed.

Do I need UTM with gclid on?

Yes — for cross‑channel consistency and BI. gclid does not conflict with UTM.

How to validate?

Click the link and verify in GA4 Realtime/DebugView; check redirects keep parameters.

Where to store taxonomy?

In a shared registry (Notion/Sheet/Git) with medium dictionary, naming rules and campaign archive.

Pre‑launch checklist

  • Lowercase/encoding, unified dictionaries.
  • Identical utm_campaign across all creatives.
  • Redirects verified and a final click test.

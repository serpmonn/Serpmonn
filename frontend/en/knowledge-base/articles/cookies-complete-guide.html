What are Cookies: Complete Guide to Management and Security

October 29, 2024 • 12 min read

Every time you visit a website, you are greeted with a message about cookie usage. But what are they really? In this guide, we will analyze everything about cookie files: from basics to advanced security settings.

What are Cookie Files?

Cookies are small text files that websites save on your device. They contain information about your actions on the internet and help websites "remember" you.

Simple Analogy

Imagine that cookies are a business card you leave at a cafe. On your next visit, the waiter recognizes you and remembers what coffee you prefer.

Identification

The website recognizes you on subsequent visits

Shopping Cart

Saves items even if you leave the website

Settings

Remembers language, currency and other preferences

How Do Cookies Work?

  1. First Visit to a Website

    When you first visit a website, the server sends a cookie file to your browser.

  2. Information Storage

    The browser saves cookies on your device. This usually happens in a special folder.

  3. Subsequent Visits

    On your next visit, the browser sends cookies back to the server so the website can "recognize" you.

Cookie File Example:

{
    "name": "session_id",
    "value": "a1b2c3d4e5f6g7h8",
    "domain": "example.com",
    "path": "/",
    "expires": "2024-11-29T10:30:00.000Z",
    "secure": true,
    "httpOnly": true
}

️ Attention: Third-party Cookies

Third-party cookies are often used to track your activity across different websites. Modern browsers are gradually phasing out their support to protect privacy.

Why Clean Cookies?

Security

Protection against session theft and unauthorized access

Privacy

Removal of tracking cookies and browsing history

Free Up Space

Cookies take up space on your device

Situations When You Need to Clean Cookies:

  • Login problems on websites
  • Slow performance of the browser
  • Incorrect display of websites
  • Selling or transferring the device
  • Suspicious activity on accounts

How to Clean Cookies

Google Chrome

  1. Open Settings → Privacy and Security
  2. Select Cookies and Site Data
  3. Click Delete All Cookies
  4. Confirm the action

Mozilla Firefox

  1. Go to Settings → Privacy & Security
  2. In the "Cookies and Site Data" section, click Delete Data
  3. Check Cookies and Site Data
  4. Click Delete

🅰️ Apple Safari

  1. Open Safari Settings
  2. Go to the Privacy tab
  3. Click Manage Website Data
  4. Select sites and click Remove or Remove All

Professional Advice

Use incognito/private browsing mode. In this mode, cookies are automatically deleted after closing the window.

Quick Cleanup with Keyboard Shortcuts:

  • Ctrl+Shift+Delete (Windows/Linux) - opens the history cleanup window
  • Cmd+Shift+Delete (Mac) - similar function in macOS

️ Cookie Management: Best Practices

  1. Block Third-party Cookies

    Enable third-party cookie blocking in your browser settings. This will significantly improve your privacy.

  2. Use Whitelist

    Allow cookies only for trusted websites that you frequently visit.

  3. Regular Cleanup

    Set a reminder to clean cookies every 1-3 months depending on activity.

  4. Management Extensions

    Use extensions like "Cookie AutoDelete" for automatic management.

️ Extensions for Cookie Management

  • Cookie AutoDelete - automatically deletes cookies from closed tabs
  • uBlock Origin - blocks trackers and advertising cookies
  • Privacy Badger - automatically blocks invisible trackers

Frequently Asked Questions

🤔 Will cleaning cookies cause problems with websites?

Yes, temporarily. After cleaning cookies, you will need to log in again to websites where you were authorized. Settings (language, theme) will also be reset.

🔒 Can cookies contain viruses?

No. Cookies are simple text files that cannot contain executable code. However, they can be used for tracking and session theft.

📱 Do I need to clean cookies on mobile devices?

Yes. Mobile browsers also use cookies, and they should be periodically cleaned to free up space and protect privacy.

⚡ Do cookies slow down browser performance?

They can. A large number of cookies can slightly slow down browser performance, especially on older devices.

🌍 What is GDPR and cookies?

GDPR is a European data protection regulation that requires websites to obtain your consent before setting cookies.

Key Takeaways:

  • Cookies are necessary for convenient internet work
  • Regular cleaning improves security and privacy
  • Blocking third-party cookies protects against tracking
  • Use modern browsers with enhanced privacy protection
  • Always read cookie usage policies on websites
